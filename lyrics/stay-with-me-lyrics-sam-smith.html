"Stay with Me" is a song by English recording artist Sam Smith from his debut studio album "In the Lonely Hour" (2014).

It was written by Sam Smith, James Napier, William Phillips, with additional credits to Tom Petty and Jeff Lynne. Producers included Jimmy Napes and Steve Fitzmaurice, as well as Rodney Jerkins for the Darkchild and Mary J. Blige version.

The song was described by Erine Keane of Salon.com as "British blue-eyed soul", while Tom Breihan of Stereogum called it "a soft-focus piano-soul ballad".

Sam Smith told 4Music "Stay With Me is my attempt at an anthem for the lonely. I wanted to write a song about those moments in the morning after a one night stand."

He also explained to NME "the song is about the moment in the morning after a one night stand, where the person you are with leaves your house, and you are left by yourself, and it's just a second, where you are just like: 'I wish, I wish'. You don't even love them, you don't really fancy them that much, it's just nice to have someone in the bed next to you."

Sam Smith revealed that the album "In The Lonely Hour" was inspired by "a guy that I fell in love with last year," but that did not reciprocate his feelings. "It’s all there now, and I can move on and hopefully find a guy who can love me the way I love him."

When Smith his Grammy Award trophy for Record of the Year, he said to the audience "I want to thank the man who this record is about, who I fell in love with last year. Thank you so much for breaking my heart, because you got me four Grammys."

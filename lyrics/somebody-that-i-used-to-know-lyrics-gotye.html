"Somebody That I Used To Know" is a song by Australian and Belgian-born artist Gotye, real name Wally De Backer, featuring New Zealand singer Kimbra. It is the second single from Gotye's third studio album, Making Mirrors (2011).
The song was written and produced by Wally De Backer, with additional credits to Luiz Bonfá.

"Somebody That I Used to Know" is a mid-tempo, indie pop ballad that samples Luiz Bonfá's song "Seville" from his 1967 album Luiz Bonfa Plays Great Songs.

In an interview with Take 40 Australia, Gotye explained: “I didn’t intend it to be a two person song from the start, but when I sort of got to the end of writing the first chorus, it didn’t feel like there was anywhere else for my character to sort of go; it didn’t feel like there was any more story to tell. So that prompted me to write an alternative perspective, the other side of the coin. I think Kimbra brought that kind of combination of angst and multiplicity through her voice, an interpretation of the lyric that made it have that edge that I hoped it would have.”

Meaning
by
AmandaH
, Editor
Apr 05 2016
While on tour in 1993, The Cranberries penned "Zombie" in memory of Jonathan Ball and Tim Parry, who were killed in an IRA bombing in Warrington. It became known as their 'protest song,' and many music critics didn't agree with the band's choice to get political in their lyrics. As Nina Simone says, though, it's an artist's job to reflect the times in their work, and "Zombie" has The Cranberries' signature song.
